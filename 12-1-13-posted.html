Sometimes a description will be clearly appropriate or inappropriate, but sometimes it will not. For example, "All of the red dots are on the right side of the picture" is not a great description of this picture, but you might feel that "There are five red dots" is OK (but not great). All such responses are useful information for us. When you feel that a description is clearly appropriate or inappropriate, choose an extreme value. If you feel that a description is somewhat, but not fully, appropriate, indicate your intuition by selecting an intermediate value.
